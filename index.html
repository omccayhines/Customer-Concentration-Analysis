High-Level Business Steps

1
Define the Risk Question
expand_more

Primary Question: How concentrated is P14's customer base, and what's our risk exposure if key customers leave?

Sub-Questions to Consider:

  • What percentage of P14 revenue comes from the top 5 customers?
  • How many customers would we need to lose to significantly impact profitability?
  • Is the customer base diversified or concentrated?
  • What's the HHI (concentration index) for P14's customer base?

Strategic Context: In M&A due diligence, customer concentration >25% (top 5 customers) often triggers 10-30% valuation discounts. Understanding concentration is critical for investment decisions.

2
Gather Customer-Level Data
expand_more

Required Data Sources:

  • Sales Transactions: Filter Fact_Sales for ProductID = "P14"
  • Customer Attributes: Merge with Dim_Customer for loyalty tier, acquisition channel, customer type
  • Revenue Calculation: UnitPriceLocal × Quantity per transaction
  • Transaction Frequency: Count of purchases per customer

Data Quality Checks:

  • Verify all P14 transactions have matching customer records
  • Check for duplicate customer IDs or transactions
  • Ensure currency consistency (all in USD or properly converted)
  • Validate that total P14 revenue matches expected $43,800.38
3
Calculate Customer Metrics
expand_more

Key Metrics to Calculate:

  • Revenue per Customer: SUM(UnitPriceLocal × Quantity) grouped by CustomerID
  • Customer Rank: Rank customers by revenue (1 = highest revenue)
  • Revenue Share: (Customer Revenue ÷ Total P14 Revenue) × 100
  • Cumulative Revenue %: Running sum of revenue share from rank 1 to N
  • Transaction Count: Number of P14 purchases per customer
  • Average Transaction Value: Revenue per Customer ÷ Transaction Count

Implementation Tip: Use Power Query's Group By function to aggregate at customer level, then add calculated columns for rank, share, and cumulative metrics.

4
Analyze Concentration (Pareto & HHI)
expand_more

Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule):

  • Identify what % of customers drive 80% of revenue
  • Calculate concentration ratios: CR5 (top 5), CR10 (top 10)
  • Compare to industry benchmarks (B2B: CR5 often 30-50%)

HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index):

  • Formula: HHI = Σ(Revenue Share)² for all customers
  • Interpretation: HHI < 0.15 = Low concentration | 0.15-0.25 = Moderate | >0.25 = High concentration
  • Example: If top customer has 10% share: (0.10)² = 0.01 contribution to HHI
  • Why It Matters: HHI is used by regulators (FTC) and investors to assess market/customer concentration risk
5
Assess Multivariate Context
expand_more

Beyond Revenue Concentration - The "What Else?" Framework:

  • Loyalty Tier Analysis: Are top customers Gold/Platinum (sticky) or Bronze (at-risk)?
  • Acquisition Channel: Referral customers (higher retention) vs. Walk-in (lower retention)?
  • Transaction Frequency: Repeat buyers vs. one-time purchasers?
  • Customer Profitability: Revenue - (COGS + Acquisition Cost + Servicing Cost) per customer
  • Churn Risk Indicators: Recent purchase date, declining transaction frequency, support tickets

Univariate vs. Multivariate Analysis:

  • Univariate (Misleading): "P14 has 62% margin → Safe investment"
  • Multivariate (Complete): "P14 has 62% margin + EXTREME concentration (69.5% from C077) + unknown loyalty of top customer → HIGH RISK - DO NOT INVEST without customer retention strategy"

Strategic Implication: Always ask "What else should I know?" before making decisions based on a single metric.

6
Calculate Profit Impact Scenarios
expand_more

Scenario Analysis - What If We Lose Key Customers?

  • Scenario 1: Lose top customer → Revenue impact = Top customer revenue × 62% margin
  • Scenario 2: Lose top 3 customers → Sum(Top 3 revenue) × 62% margin
  • Scenario 3: Lose top 5 customers → Sum(Top 5 revenue) × 62% margin

Example Calculation (if top customer = $650 revenue):

  • Revenue Loss: $650
  • Profit Loss: $650 × 0.62 = $403
  • % of Total P14 Profit: $403 ÷ $27,190 = 1.48% impact

Risk Assessment: If losing top 5 customers impacts >10% of profit, implement customer retention strategies immediately.

7
Develop Strategic Recommendations
expand_more

Based on Concentration Analysis, Recommend:

  • If Low Concentration (HHI < 0.15): Invest in growth and expansion; customer base is diversified
  • If Moderate Concentration (HHI 0.15-0.25): Invest with customer retention programs for top 10 customers
  • If High Concentration (HHI > 0.25): Diversify customer base before major investment; implement VIP retention programs

Actionable Strategies:

  • Customer Retention: VIP programs, dedicated account managers, loyalty rewards for top customers
  • Diversification: Target new customer segments, expand marketing to reduce dependency
  • Risk Monitoring: Quarterly HHI tracking, early warning system for customer churn
  • Contract Strategies: Long-term contracts with top customers to reduce churn risk

Deliverable: Executive summary with concentration metrics, risk assessment, and strategic recommendations.

Power Query Quick Reference

Power Query Implementation Steps
expand_more

Step 1: Load and Filter P14 Sales Data

let Source = Excel.CurrentWorkbook(){[Name="Fact_Sales"]}[Content], FilterP14 = Table.SelectRows(Source, each [ProductID] = "P14") in FilterP14

Step 2: Merge with Customer Dimension

let MergeCustomer = Table.NestedJoin(FilterP14, {"CustomerID"}, Dim_Customer, {"CustomerID"}, "Customer", JoinKind.Left), ExpandCustomer = Table.ExpandTableColumn(MergeCustomer, "Customer", {"CustomerName", "LoyaltyTier", "AcquisitionChannel", "CustomerType"}) in ExpandCustomer

Step 3: Aggregate Revenue by Customer

let GroupByCustomer = Table.Group(ExpandCustomer, {"CustomerID", "CustomerName", "LoyaltyTier", "AcquisitionChannel"}, { {"TotalRevenue", each List.Sum([UnitPriceLocal] * [Quantity]), type number}, {"TransactionCount", each Table.RowCount(_), Int64.Type}, {"AvgTransactionValue", each List.Average([UnitPriceLocal] * [Quantity]), type number} }) in GroupByCustomer

Step 4: Calculate Concentration Metrics

let SortByRevenue = Table.Sort(GroupByCustomer, {{"TotalRevenue", Order.Descending}}), AddRank = Table.AddIndexColumn(SortByRevenue, "CustomerRank", 1, 1), TotalP14Revenue = List.Sum(AddRank[TotalRevenue]), AddRevenueShare = Table.AddColumn(AddRank, "RevenueShare", each [TotalRevenue] / TotalP14Revenue), AddCumulativeShare = Table.AddColumn(AddRevenueShare, "CumulativeShare", each List.Sum(List.FirstN(AddRevenueShare[RevenueShare], [CustomerRank]))), AddHHIContribution = Table.AddColumn(AddCumulativeShare, "HHI_Contribution", each Number.Power([RevenueShare], 2)) in AddHHIContribution

Step 5: Create Risk Assessment

let AddConcentrationTier = Table.AddColumn(AddHHIContribution, "ConcentrationTier", each if [CustomerRank] <= 5 then "Top 5 - Critical" else if [CustomerRank] <= 10 then "Top 10 - High Risk" else if [CumulativeShare] <= 0.8 then "Core Base" else "Long Tail"), AddRiskFlag = Table.AddColumn(AddConcentrationTier, "RiskFlag", each if [RevenueShare] > 0.05 then "High Dependency (>5%)" else if [RevenueShare] > 0.02 then "Moderate Dependency (2-5%)" else "Low Dependency (<2%)") in AddRiskFlag

Common Pitfalls

Pitfall Why It Happens How to Avoid
Assuming Profitability = Sustainability Focusing only on profit margin without considering customer concentration risk Always analyze customer concentration alongside profitability metrics; ask "What if we lose top customers?"
The Univariate Analysis Trap Examining one variable (profit) in isolation without multivariate context (concentration, loyalty, churn) Use the "What else should I know?" framework; consider profit + concentration + loyalty + acquisition cost simultaneously
Ignoring Customer Quality Treating all customers equally without considering loyalty tier, acquisition channel, or transaction frequency Weight concentration risk by customer quality; Gold tier referral customers are stickier than Bronze walk-ins
Misinterpreting HHI Not understanding HHI scale or thresholds (0-1 range, where >0.25 = high concentration) Learn HHI benchmarks: <0.15 = Low | 0.15-0.25 = Moderate | >0.25 = High concentration
Forgetting Profit Impact Scenarios Calculating concentration % without translating to actual profit loss scenarios Always calculate: Revenue loss × Profit margin = Profit impact for top 1, 3, 5, and 10 customers
Not Considering Acquisition Cost Focusing only on current revenue without factoring in customer acquisition cost (CAC) or lifetime value (LTV) Combine concentration analysis with CAC/LTV; a high-revenue customer with high CAC may be less valuable

Pro Tips

Advanced Analysis Techniques
expand_more

M&A Due Diligence Application:

  • Valuation Impact: Customer concentration >25% (CR5) often triggers 10-30% valuation discounts
  • Due Diligence Checklist: Calculate HHI, CR5, CR10 for target company's customer base
  • Negotiation Strategy: Use concentration findings to negotiate earn-outs tied to customer retention
  • Integration Planning: High concentration = prioritize customer retention in first 100 days post-acquisition

Supplier Concentration Analysis:

  • Apply same methodology to supplier base (operational risk instead of revenue risk)
  • High supplier concentration = supply chain vulnerability and negotiation disadvantage
  • Calculate HHI for suppliers; if >0.25, develop supplier diversification strategy
  • Combine with supplier criticality assessment (concentration + single-source components = highest risk)

Customer Profitability Matrix (2×2 Framework):

  • Stars (High Concentration + High Profitability): VIP retention programs, dedicated account managers
  • Cash Cows (High Concentration + Low Profitability): Renegotiate pricing or consider exit
  • Growth Opportunities (Low Concentration + High Profitability): Expand customer base in this segment
  • Dogs (Low Concentration + Low Profitability): Deprioritize or discontinue

Monitoring and Early Warning Systems:

  • Quarterly HHI Tracking: Set up automated dashboards to monitor concentration trends
  • Alert Thresholds: Trigger alerts when HHI increases by >0.05 or any customer exceeds 10% of revenue
  • Churn Prediction: Monitor transaction frequency, support tickets, payment delays for top customers
  • Proactive Retention: Implement retention campaigns before customers show churn signals

Geographic and Product Concentration:

  • Extend analysis to geographic markets (revenue concentration by region/country)
  • Analyze product concentration (% of revenue from top 5 products)
  • Calculate multi-dimensional HHI (customer × geography × product)
  • Diversification strategies should address all concentration dimensions simultaneously

Real-World Example - Tech Startup Valuation:

  • Scenario: SaaS startup with $10M ARR, 70% gross margin, seeking Series B funding
  • Initial Valuation: $50M (5x revenue multiple)
  • Due Diligence Finding: Top 3 customers = 45% of ARR (HHI = 0.28 - high concentration)
  • Investor Response: Valuation reduced to $35M (3.5x revenue) due to concentration risk
  • Outcome: Startup implemented customer diversification strategy, reduced HHI to 0.12 over 18 months
  • Series C Result: Raised at 6x revenue ($60M valuation) with improved concentration profile
  • Lesson: Customer concentration directly impacts valuation - diversification creates shareholder value

LTV-Weighted HHI (Advanced Metric):

  • Concept: Weight concentration by customer lifetime value (LTV) instead of current revenue
  • Formula: LTV-HHI = Σ(LTV Share)² for all customers
  • Why It Matters: High-concentration customers with high LTV are less risky than those with low LTV
  • Application: Adjust concentration risk assessments based on customer stickiness and retention rates
